# wellX — Athletes Choice Massage

A/B test landing pages for **Athletes Choice Massage** (ACM), driven by Meta ads. Single conversion goal: book online via
`https://app.hivemanager.io/book/athleteschoicemassage`. For TMJ-specific landings, append `?category=Mh3KjJu8yr`.

ACM is locally-owned, partner-run, Edmonton-based (4 locations), in business since 2016. Brand fundamentals (colors, fonts, voice, slogan, persona, photography rules) live in `CLAUDE.md` and the brand book PDF in this directory — this file captures the *design context* layered on top of those fundamentals.

---

## Design Context

### Users

The primary persona is **Jessica** (per the brand book): 32yo professional, desk-bound work, runs + does yoga, has insurance, books for pain relief, scrolls TikTok/Instagram ~2.5 hrs/day. She's active but not hardcore — she's building healthy long-term routines, not training for a competition.

For the TMJ landing page specifically: same persona, but the click intent is jaw tension. She probably wakes up with a sore jaw or dull headache, has read about TMJ online, may have a night guard already, and arrives from a phone scroll between meetings or at night.

She wants to know:
- *Is this a real thing they actually treat, or is it just generic massage?*
- *Will they understand what's happening in my jaw?*
- *Can I book quickly without a phone call or referral?*

### Brand voice (designer's distillation)

The brand book is unambiguous: **"trusted coach or teammate."** That single phrase governs everything.

- Encouraging, motivating, but never cheerleading or performative.
- Knowledgeable, but not above the reader. The coach explains the body without lecturing.
- **Empowering** — language gives the client agency: "You can," "We'll help you," "Here's what's happening so you can make the call."
- **Never** spa: no "relax," "treat yourself," "pamper," "escape," "indulge," "moment for you," "self-care ritual."
- **Never** clinical-cold: no "patients," no "presenting symptoms," no "modality."

### Aesthetic direction

The brand fundamentals (Vivid Yellow `#FFD600` + Flint `#716B64` + Montserrat) point to a **bright, confident, modern-clinic** aesthetic — visually closer to a community gym or PT clinic than a spa or a luxury wellness brand.

- **Theme: light, paper-warm background.** Off-white tinted with the brand's warm gray hue. Never sterile pure white. Never dark-mode by default. Use Flint as a *secondary* dark surface for emphasis sections (e.g., the "Is this you" panel).
- **Yellow is loud — use sparingly.** Saved for primary CTAs (yellow background, black text), section accents (small yellow dot, underline highlight on a key word), and one or two slogan moments. **Never** as text on light backgrounds.
- **Typography**: Montserrat across the entire weight range (400, 600, 700, 800, 900). Hierarchy comes from weight + size + tracking, not from a second font. Display headlines use 800–900 with very tight tracking. Body uses 400 with comfortable leading.
- **Layout** is editorial-modern — generous whitespace, varied rhythm, asymmetric compositions, full-width restraint. Not card-grid heavy.
- **Photography direction** (when real photos arrive): low light, high contrast, real practitioners in ACM-branded shirts working on real clients. Until those exist, the page can use anatomical reference imagery (Gray's Anatomy plates, public domain) styled with `mix-blend-mode: multiply` to integrate with the warm paper background.

### Explicitly NOT

- Spa tropes (lotus, stones, candles, script fonts, dim purple/teal, towels-in-basket photography)
- Sterile cyan-and-white medical-template look
- Dark-mode-with-glowing-yellow neon energy
- AI-slop landing page patterns: gradient headline text, glowing cyan-on-dark hero, border-left accent stripes on cards, identical three-up feature card grids, glassmorphism, floating orb backgrounds
- Yellow as text on light backgrounds (always fails contrast)
- Generic massage-therapy hands-on-neck stock photography

### Design principles

1. **Coach the visitor.** Every paragraph should feel like a knowledgeable teammate explaining the body. Inform > impress.
2. **Empower, don't pamper.** Write copy that returns agency — "here's what's happening, here's how we help, here's what you do next." Never "let us take care of you."
3. **Yellow is a precision tool.** It's the loudest color in the system; deploy it where you want the eye to go (CTAs, slogan, key emphasis), nowhere else.
4. **One path forward.** Single, unambiguous CTA: book. Every section either builds toward it or clears a doubt that's blocking it.
5. **Mobile-first, thumb-first.** Meta traffic arrives on phones, often after-hours. Big tap targets, scannable sections, CTA always within thumb reach.
6. **Specifics earn trust.** Name the muscles, name the techniques, name the locations. Vagueness is the spa's tool, not ours.

### Accessibility

WCAG AA as baseline. Color contrast verified for every text/background pair (yellow text gets aggressive contrast checking — usually means yellow goes on flint or near-black, not on paper). `prefers-reduced-motion` respected. Tap targets ≥ 44px. Readable at 200% zoom.

### A/B testing

Two landing pages live in the project. Both share the brand foundation above. They diverge intentionally on hero angle, copy strategy, or layout — the differences should be material enough to actually learn from, not just typography swaps. Confirm the A/B differentiator with the user before building variant B.
